Southern Charm's Madison LeCroy admits she underwent plastic surgery including boob job and 'putting

Publish date: 2024-06-07

SOUTHERN Charm's Madison LeCroy came clean about her plastic surgery journey after she went under the knife for a "full mommy makeover."

The reality star has been very open about her cosmetic work, keeping her followers informed throughout the procedure and post-op.

Madison, 31, recently spoke to Bravo Insider about her makeover, where she hoped to "tweak" her body "just a bit."

The TV star told the platform: "I wanted to open up the conversation, because I feel like people think that [plastic surgery] is such an easy way out."

The TV personality wanted to make it clear that insecurities are not eliminated after altering your looks.

She said: “But the thing was, I put in so much work. I worked out, I ate well, and there were things that I just could not change. And that is because I had a ten-pound baby, and every mother knows that things are very different after that.”

Though this was her first procedure of the kind, Madison entrusted Charleston-based surgeon, Dr. Patrick J. O’Neill, to get the job done.

She explained: “He was realistic. He’s like, ‘I don’t think you need that,’ or, ‘I think this is gonna fit you.'

“I just wanted to make sure that it was tasteful, and I was happy. I don’t want people to look at my chest before they look at me as an individual, and be like, ‘Oh my god.’” 

After her surgery was complete, the mom of one recalled feeling "loopy" from the anesthesia and thinking “what have I done?”

Though after recovery she admitted the pain was "not that bad," she did note: “having my abdominal muscles put back together was probably one of the worst pains I’ve ever been in.”

In regards to the results, Madison is very pleased, telling Bravo:  “I’m very happy with my results.

"And I recommend it for anyone who has insecurities about something that they try to fix over and over. But, I recommend putting in the hard work before you go through a surgery.”

When asked if she will return to the surgeon's chair, the bright blonde was not convinced.

She said: “I think I’m done. I mean, I’m sure when I get older, there may be things that I’ll end up doing. Like yes, I do get Botox in my forehead.

"Do I mess with anything else in my face? I don’t. I’m not gonna do anything.

"I’m not gonna try to fix my crooked lip that I was born with, ‘cause that’s just who I am. That’s like a part of me."

She added: "I’m okay with that. I’m okay with imperfections.”
