Maggie Smith bio, marriage, education, career, net worth

Publish date: 2024-05-21

Who is Maggie Smith?

Maggie Smith is a British actress most known for her part in “The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie,” which won her an Academy Award and her appearances in the Harry Potter series and “Downton Abbey.”

Maggie Smith biography

On December 28, 1934, Margaret Natalie “Maggie” Smith was born in Ilford, Essex. Her mother’s name was Margaret Hutton, and her father was a pathologist who specialized in public health.

Her family relocated to Oxford when she was 4 years old since her father was employed there as a pathologist by Oxford University.

In 1990, Smith received the title of Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire.

Smith received a diagnosis of breast cancer in 2008 and underwent extensive treatment, including chemotherapy sessions, which fell during the Half-Blood Prince filming schedule.

Maggie Smith education

Smith attended the Oxford Playhouse School from 1951 to 1953 after finishing high school. She made her professional stage debut in 1952, playing Viola in a performance of Twelfth Night by the Oxford University Dramatics Society.

Maggie Smith marriage

Smith has had two marriages. Her first union, which she had in 1967 with actor Robert Stephens, ended in divorce in 1974. Toby Stephens and Chris Larkin, her two sons with Stephens, are both actors. Smith married the author Beverley Cross in 1975; she passed away from cancer in 1998.

Maggie smith career

Smith started acting in films after a brief detour on Broadway in New York, when she appeared in the comic revue New Faces in 1956. Her first acting gig was a brief, uncredited cameo in the 1956 film Child in the House, which was followed by a bigger role in the 1959 crime drama Nowhere to Go.

One of Maggie Smith’s most notable novels is “The Prime of Miss Jean Brodie.” The movie, which was directed by Ronald Neame, was adapted from Muriel Spark’s book of the same name.

Smith won the Oscar for Best Actress for her portrayal of an idealistic, unconventional schoolteacher. She also took home a BAFTA in the same field. Although the movie didn’t do well monetarily, critics gave it favorable reviews.
