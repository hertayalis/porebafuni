Who is Javier Bardem?

Javier Ángel Encinas Bardem akak Javier Bardem is a Spanish actor. Bardem won the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for his role as the psychopathic assassin Anton Chigurh in the 2007 Coen brothers film No Country for Old Men.

Javier Bardem’s Early Life, Education, and Childhood

Javier Bardem was born on 1 March 1969 in Spain. He was the son of the actress Pilar Bardem and businessman José Carlos. He belongs to the acting family.

When he was six years old, he appeared in his first feature, “El Picaro”. During his teenage years, he acted in several TV series, played rugby for the Spanish National Team, and toured the country with an independent theatrical group.

In 1989, for the Spanish comedy show El Día Por Delante, he had to wear a Superman costume for a comedic sketch, a job that made him question whether he wanted to be an actor at all.

He had confessed to having worked as a stripper during his struggling acting career.

Javier Bardem’s Career, Salary, and Net Worth

Javier Bardem started his career but played a small role in his first major motion picture, The Ages of Lulu when he was 20. He had appeared along with his mother, Pilar Bardem.

Bigas Luna, the director of Lulu in 1992. In 1993, he starred again in Luna’s next film Golden Balls. Similarly, in 1997, he was the first to approach him, then a 27-year-old, for a role in English, but the Spanish actor turned down the offer because his English was still poor.

In 1999, Bardem starred with Spanish siren Victoria Abril in Entre las piranas. Then he also went on to tackle a prominent role in actor John Malkovich’s directorial debut, The Dancer Upstairs in 2002.

In 2007, Bardem appeared in two literary adaptations: Love in the Time of Cholera and No Country for Old Men. He had starred with Penelope Cruz again in Woody Allen’s popular 2008 film, Vicky Cristina Barcelona.

Javier Bardem later played a leading role in the 2010 film Eat Pray Love. In 2010, he was also awarded Best Actor at the Cannes Film Festival for his performance in Biutiful directed by Alejandro González Iñárritu, who specifically wrote the film with Bardem in mind.
